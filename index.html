moment 01 · the ask

One question a day, not a stock-take.

Perpetual inventory drifts; full counts burn hours. The brain derives on-hand from sales and deliveries, tracks its own doubt per ingredient (value at risk × days since a human confirmed), and spends its one daily question where doubt × money is highest. The answer corrects the books and re-anchors the derivation.

the queue is ranked, not random: 5 open questions across 38 tracked items in the demo world. answering one visibly re-ranks the rest.

moment 02 · the order

The order drafts itself — from the forecast, not a par sheet.

Every night: forecast tomorrow's demand from the venue's learned rhythm, subtract what's on hand, respect pack sizes, and price each line from the latest invoice actually paid. The draft lands consolidated per supplier; the manager amends or approves. Every amend is recorded as a correction the brain learns from.

38 lines across 5 suppliers in tonight's draft. approve is idempotent — a double-tap can never order twice.

moment 03 · the door

Invoices are data, not paperwork.

A photo at the back door is read line-by-line and echoed back verbatim — what the brain read is what you see, never a summary it composed. The prices land on the books and feed three things at once: the € on the next draft order, the overpay checks, and the food-cost ledger.

in production the reader is an LLM extractor behind a fail-loud seam; in this static demo the extract is assembled from the order book (labelled below).

moment 04 · the grade

It predicts with a band — then publishes whether reality landed inside.

Every forecast ships with an 80%-confidence band and becomes a row in a decision ledger: forecasts, order drafts, drift flags, spot-checks — 27 decisions in the demo world, each graded once reality arrives. The report card replays the whole history and states the coverage. Misses aren't hidden; the worst one is the headline.

n is small and the page says so — 13 banded forecasts is a baseline, not a brag. the discipline of grading is the product; the number just has to be honest.

moment 05 · the group

Each venue gets its own brain. The group gets the exceptions.

Venues genuinely differ — one is flat all week, the next peaks Saturdays — so each learns its own rhythm, pars and prices. The group tier sees the league table (food cost, waste, coverage) and only the things worth a human's attention: a band miss, an overpay, drift. Everything else stays quietly handled below.

what this demo is — exactly

Honest about its own seams.

  • Every number you scrolled past is real engine output — captured byte-for-byte from the production API running over a seeded, fictional tenant ("Burger House", world frozen at 2026-05-31). Nothing on this page was composed by hand.
  • The app behind the buttons is the real frontend, built statically: reads replay those captures; the five write loops (counts, sends, corrections, invoice upload) are simulated in-browser against the same rules the backend applies.
  • The invoice "OCR" in this demo assembles its extract from the order book — the production extractor is an LLM behind a fail-loud seam, not shipped in a static page.
  • The report card says "small n" because n is small. 13 banded forecasts is a calibration baseline. The claim is the discipline — forecast, band, grade, publish — not the percentage.
  • The platform's source is private; this page and the built bundle are the showcase. The logic is documented in the README.
